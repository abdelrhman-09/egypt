Welcome to Egypt

Discover the land of pharaohs, pyramids, and timeless wonders

Egypt, a country linking northeast Africa with the Middle East, dates to the time of the pharaohs. Millennia-old monuments sit along the fertile Nile River Valley, including Giza's colossal Pyramids and Great Sphinx as well as Luxor's hieroglyph-lined Karnak Temple and Valley of the Kings tombs.

Must-Visit Places in Egypt

Pyramids of Giza

The last surviving wonder of the ancient world, the Pyramids of Giza have been astonishing visitors since they were built over 4,500 years ago.

Luxor Temple

This large Ancient Egyptian temple complex located on the east bank of the Nile River was founded in 1400 BCE.

Abu Simbel

The twin temples carved out of mountainside during the reign of Pharaoh Ramesses II in the 13th century BCE.

Alexandria

Egypt's second-largest city with a Mediterranean atmosphere, home to the famous Bibliotheca Alexandrina.

Red Sea Riviera

Famous for its spectacular coral reefs and beach resorts like Sharm El Sheikh and Hurghada.

Siwa Oasis

A tranquil oasis near the Libyan border, known for its olive and date groves and unique Berber culture.

Delicious Egyptian Cuisine

Koshary

Egypt's national dish - a mix of rice, lentils, chickpeas, pasta, and fried onions topped with tomato sauce and garlic vinegar.

Ful Medames

A staple breakfast dish made from fava beans cooked with oil and salt, typically served with bread.

Molokhia

A green soup made from jute leaves, usually served with rabbit or chicken and rice or bread.

Hawawshi

A pita bread stuffed with minced meat and spices then baked or grilled to perfection.

Fatta

A festive dish made of rice, fried bread, and meat in a garlic-vinegar sauce, traditionally served on special occasions.

Umm Ali

Egypt's famous bread pudding dessert made with pastry, milk, nuts, and raisins.
